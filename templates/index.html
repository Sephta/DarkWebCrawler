Dark Web Crawler


Problem
The websites in the Dark Web are characterized by their use of encryption software that makes their users and their locations anonymous. The fact that data can be transferred anonymously is why illegal activity is so common on the Dark web. However, itâ€™s also used by political whistle-blowers, activists, and journalists who may be censored or could risk political retaliation if discovered by their government. Our primary goal is to provide insight into the composition of the dark web while being transparent about our methodology as well as describing the security risks of accessing the Dark Web. Due to our limited access and research timeframe, being rigorous and transparent about how we aggregate data and acknowledging the inherent biases of our methodology are essential for us to provide accurate and detailed accounts of what we have found.

FAQ Section
What is the Dark Web?
The Dark Web is the part of the internet that is hidden from public view. The Dark Web is much larger then the internet you can access using Google and runs on encrypted browers such that users are anonymous and can't be tracked.
What are the risks of accessing the Dark Web?
Unlike browsers such as Google Chrome or Firefox which protect and monitor users, the dark web browsers such as Tor will keep users anonymous which attracts people who may want to hack you, buy from illegal markets or view illegal sites with little to no reprecussions.
Is the Dark Web safe to access?
Short answer, no. Unless you know how to protect yourself. Dark Web browers don't have the same level of protection as public browers such as Google or Bing.
Why would someone want to access the Dark Web?
The main reason that someone would access the dark Web is to search for, or participate in illegal activities. You could find stolen movies or music, drugs, credit card numbers, personal identifiable information (SSN, address, phone numbers) or see if your own information is floating around out there.
